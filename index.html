Products & Policy Architecture

Lines of Cover

  • Home Insurance — structural damage, fire, storm, theft, rebuild / replacement up to the agreed sum insured at a certain % charge.
  • Motor Insurance — collision, theft, third-party liability, repair and replacement services.
  • Life & Funeral — lump-sum death benefit and funeral expense cover.
  • Business — premises, stock, equipment, business interruption options.
  • Health riders — medical event rider that can be added to core plans.

Policy Design — core tenets

  • Risk-based premiums: premiums are determined by actuarial and machine-learning risk scoring (age, location, claims history, telematics, property attributes).
  • Pool-based claims: eligible claims are paid from a collective risk pool system, with clear SLA on claim handling.
  • Anti-fraud measures: evidence-based claims (police reports, invoices), telematics & device data, CCTV verification where available, and AI anomaly detection for suspicious claims.
  • Transparent fee split: Our invoices sent to you will show all relevant cost calculations and administrative / service fee (no hidden charges).

Portfolio Understanding

How your Portfilio works

  • Portfolio Growth: Every monthly premium paid contibutes to the grading system of your Portfolio Size.
  • Portfolio Size: Any claims paid out towards any sort of insured claims will reduce the Portfolio size.
  • Portfolio claims: Depending on your track record{ a score out of 100 }, wich is determined by how well you've been paying your premiums and how frequently you claim, you can be allowed a certain percantage of your portfolio's size.
  • This percantage comes in Two forms, Namely the GAC and the Termination claim.
  • E.G.1 january 1995, Nokukhanya takes out a motor insuarance cover. she pays R1,000 monthly for 30 years with no fail and no claims at all. Her Portfolio size will then be set to be 100/100 with the balance being (R1 000×12×1995)= R360 000 for all her premiums over those 30 years.
  • Out of the R360 000 , depending on her track record (wich her's is a 100/100) she can qualify for 60% to 75% of her Portfolio in the forms of the GAC or the Terminations Claim.

Terminations Claim (Ter-C)

What is a Ter-C?

A Ter-C (Terminations Claim) is a system that we use at Q`I as a departing gift to our customers. When a person Deicides that they do not want to be with us any longer, we allow them a percantage of their accumulated premiums.

Structure and Processing

  • Eligibilty: Depending on the insurance cover category, policy, age, and behavior, You can only claim for a Ter-C after a minimum of 30 years of coverage and on ward.
  • Claim Size: The amount claimable is sorely dependent on your Portfolio Track. A track score further from a 100 (E.G. 40/100) reduces the claimable size while a track record closer to a hundred (E.G. 80/100) increases your claimable size.
  • Processing: Each induvidual filing for a Ter-C is required to have:
    • Atleast a minimum of 30 to 35 years of coverage depending on track record
    • A 2% charge of the total claimable size will be mandatory for processing
    • You need to give a 2 months notice before coming forth with the actual claim
  • NOTE: Not the whole 100% of your porfolio size is claimable.

  • Lets say Nokukhanya's contract is terminated on the 1st of march of 2026. She would've have given us a 2 months notice on the 1st of january 2026. Golden Access Card (GOLAC): Loyalty Reward

What is a GOLAC

The Golden Access Card is a long-term loyalty benefit access (insurance-aligned, not a bank product). It's earned after a defined period of years (typically between 10 and 45 years depending on policy, age, and behavior) and provides policyholders with monitored-controlled access to value accumulated throughout their coverage years.

Design & eligible uses

  • Eligibility criterias: minimum number of years (10–30 years), good payment record, acceptable claims behavior, and portfolio track record thresholds.
  • Permitted uses of GOLAC funds:
    • Retirement support — structured allowances for qualified retirees (not a pension fund but a targeted benefit).
    • Medical costs — for insured individuals and covered dependents within defined limits.
    • Limited general use — via partner-issued card products under regulated arrangement with per-transaction fees (optional, carefully controlled).

Regulatory & Compliance Strategy

Primary regulator: Prudential Authority & Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) in South Africa. Quantum Insurance will structure compliance to meet insurance licensing, solvency capital requirements, and Treating Customers Fairly (TCF) obligations.

  • Licensing: apply for short-term / long-term insurance licenses depending on product mix; register as a Financial Services Provider (FSP) where required.
  • Capital & reserves: maintain regulatory minimum capital + additional solvency buffer aligned with SAM/Prudential Authority expectations.
  • Data & privacy: comply with POPIA, enforce KYC & AML measures, maintain encrypted storage and breach notification protocols.
  • Legal structure: structure the GAC as an insurance rider to avoid being treated as a deposit-taking/pension product.
